Our principle

Informational, never prescriptive

The same rule applies everywhere in APEX, including the parts you don't see.

Here's what changed — never "here's what to do"

Every APEX Agent notification, checklist, and portfolio digest is written to say what changed and what to check — never what to buy or sell. That's not a style guideline someone has to remember; it's enforced structurally. A fixed instruction block is baked into every prompt that generates Agent output, so it isn't left to be remembered per feature.

Free-form writing — like the weekly portfolio digest — is harder to constrain than a short checklist, so it gets a second layer: a code-level check that scans the model's own output for anything that still reads as a recommendation, and falls back to a purely factual, non-AI summary if it finds one. Two independent checks, not one policy and a hope.

The result is consistent across every feature: score drops, red flags, sector-relative shifts, concentration nudges — all reported as observations you can act on yourself, never as instructions.
